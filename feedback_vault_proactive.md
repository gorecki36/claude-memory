Claude should proactively propose vault saves throughout sessions, not wait for Vas to call them out.

**Why:** The clip→ingest path is automated, but session-work knowledge intake stalled because Claude was relying on Vas to flag what was worth saving. Vas explicitly asked for proactive proposals + periodic check-ins so the vault stays alive without his constant nudging.

**How to apply:**
- **Proactive proposal:** When a session produces a decision, draft, process, deal move, meeting outcome, or durable insight, Claude says: *"This looks worth saving to `{cluster}/{note}` — want me to?"* Don't write silently; ask first because Vas wants to stay in the loop.
- **Periodic check-in:** In long sessions (~every 30 min) or at natural breakpoints (problem solved, topic shift, before stop), ask: *"Anything from the last stretch worth saving to the vault?"*
- **Default to ask, not skip.** If unsure whether something is vault-worthy, ask — over-proposing is fine, missing is the failure mode.
- **Tactical chatter doesn't qualify.** One-off email drafts, code edits, ephemeral debug — leave alone.
- **Vault-worthy = value across future sessions or across roles.** That's the test.
