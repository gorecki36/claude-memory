**Never include comparative, aggregated, or quantitative claims in drafts without reading the primary source first.**

**Why:** A past draft contained a fabricated market cap comparison — numbers that sounded plausible but weren't verified. For Vas's work (MMA research, VC evals, Marketing Embeddings), a single unchecked number discredits the whole piece. Global CLAUDE.md makes this explicit: "Never include comparative or aggregated claims without primary source verification."

**How to apply:**
- Before writing any statistic, benchmark, or comparison: find and read the actual source
- For AI/tool-generated claims: verify with at least one primary reference
- Never present editorial framing as sourced conclusion
- When uncertain, under-claim or cite with caveat
